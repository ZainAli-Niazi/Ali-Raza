WEBP to JPEG Conversion Tips

Learn how to get the best results when converting between these modern and traditional image formats

June 5, 2024

When to Use JPEG

JPEG is ideal for photographs and complex images with many colors. It offers good compression while maintaining decent quality, making it perfect for web use and sharing.

Tip: Use quality setting between 70–80 for optimal balance.
May 22, 2024

Quality Comparison

WEBP typically offers better compression than JPEG at similar quality levels. However, JPEG remains the most universally compatible format across all devices and browsers.

Tip: WEBP can be 25–35% smaller than JPEG at same quality.
May 10, 2024

Conversion Best Practices

When converting WEBP to JPEG, consider the final use case. For web, 80% quality is often sufficient. For print, use higher quality settings (90–100%).

Tip: Avoid multiple JPEG saves as quality degrades each time.
June 12, 2024

Device Compatibility

While WEBP is gaining support, some older devices and applications still don't support it. Converting to JPEG ensures your images work everywhere.

Tip: Check your audience's devices before choosing formats.
