Compatibility Sharing

When to Convert AVIF to JPEG

While AVIF offers superior compression, JPEG remains the universal standard. Convert when you need maximum compatibility with older devices, social media platforms, or when file size is less critical than support.

Most social media platforms still convert uploaded images to JPEG automatically.
July 5, 2024
Quality Compression

Quality Comparison

AVIF can be 50% smaller than JPEG at similar quality levels for photos. However, JPEG quality at 80-90% is often indistinguishable from AVIF for most viewing scenarios while maintaining wider compatibility.

For web use, JPEG quality of 75-85% provides the best balance.
June 28, 2024
Devices Browsers

Device Compatibility

As of 2024, AVIF support is growing but still not universal. Older smartphones (pre-2020), some browsers, and many applications still don't support AVIF. JPEG works everywhere without exception.

Check your audience's devices before choosing AVIF for critical images.
June 20, 2024
Photography Printing

Photography Considerations

For professional photography, AVIF preserves more detail in shadows and highlights. When converting to JPEG, use the highest quality setting (90-100%) to minimize quality loss in these areas.

For print photography, always use JPEG quality 95-100%.
June 15, 2024
Performance Audience

Web Performance

While AVIF files are smaller, the decoding time can be longer than JPEG on older devices. For websites targeting diverse audiences, JPEG may provide better overall performance.

Test both formats with your actual audience for the best results.
June 10, 2024
Editing Workflow

Editing Workflow

Edit images in AVIF format when possible to preserve maximum quality, then export final versions to JPEG for distribution. Avoid multiple JPEG re-saves as quality degrades each time.

Maintain AVIF originals and create JPEG copies as needed.
